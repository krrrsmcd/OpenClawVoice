The Quiet Power of Reading With Your Ears

For most of history, stories were heard before they were read. Long before printing presses and paperbacks, people gathered to listen. There is something in that older habit worth reclaiming.

Listening to an article while you walk changes your relationship to it. You cannot skim. You cannot skip ahead to the conclusion. You simply move at the pace of the voice, and in doing so you often notice arguments and turns of phrase you would have rushed past on the page.

This short passage exists for one reason: to prove that a written paragraph can become a natural, human-sounding voice you can carry in your pocket. If you are hearing these words, the first piece works.
